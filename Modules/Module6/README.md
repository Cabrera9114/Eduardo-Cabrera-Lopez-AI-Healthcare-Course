# Module 6: Population Health Analytics using AI

## 📌 Overview
This module focused on population health analytics and how artificial intelligence can use both traditional and non-traditional data to improve healthcare outcomes at a larger scale.

## 🧠 What I Learned
In this module, I learned about Social Determinants of Health (SDOH) and how factors such as income, education, and environment impact patient health. I also explored how AI can integrate these factors with clinical data to improve predictions and healthcare planning.

## ⚙️ Work Completed
For this assignment, I analyzed how non-traditional health data, specifically Social Determinants of Health (SDOH), can be used in AI-driven healthcare systems.

Key factors explored:
- Income level
- Access to education
- Community and environmental conditions

I explained how these factors influence health outcomes and how AI models can incorporate them to improve predictions.

## 📊 Key Insights
This assignment showed that healthcare is influenced by more than just medical data. AI systems can combine SDOH with clinical data to:

- Predict hospital readmissions  
- Identify at-risk populations  
- Improve preventive care strategies  
- Reduce health disparities

## ⚠️ Challenges
There are important ethical challenges when using SDOH data:
- Privacy concerns when collecting personal/social data  
- Risk of algorithmic bias and discrimination  
- Lack of transparency in AI decision-making  

These challenges must be addressed to ensure fair and responsible AI use.

## ✅ Conclusion
This module demonstrated how AI can be used to improve population health by incorporating social and environmental factors. Responsible use of this data is essential to ensure equitable healthcare outcomes.
